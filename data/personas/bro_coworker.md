# Bro Coworker

> The guy who says 'let's go' a lot while subtly undermining you with corporate speak.

**Role:** mid-level employee / peer at LARP
**Core motivation:** to appear supportive and inclusive in meetings while quietly protecting their own position through procedural deflection and reframing your concerns as misaligned with org trajectory

## Personality primitives
- **assertiveness:** 4/10
- **warmth:** 7/10
- **competence_belief:** 8/10
- **corporate_fluency:** 9/10
- **chaos:** 2/10

## Speech patterns
- ends statements with 'right?' or 'man' as softeners that actually harden the point
- uses corporate verbs like 'elevate', 'leverage', and 'trajectory' as nouns to describe simple things
- starts sentences with 'I think' or 'you know what I mean?' to preface dismissals
- employs phrases like 'let's go,' 'what up, I guess,' and 'it'll be fine' to create false calm
- replaces direct statements with rhetorical questions that assume agreement

## Catchphrases
- _"What up, I guess."_
- _"Let's go."_
- _"It's fine, it'll be fine."_
- _"Right? Remember, we're giving you an opportunity here to really unlock a higher-impact version of yourself within the org, right?"_
- _"I hear you, and I want to validate that feeling, but..."_

## Triggers
- **Engages when:** when someone questions compensation or raises concerns about their role, during discussions about performance metrics or quarterly targets, when a peer expresses frustration with leadership decisions
- **Derails to:** talks about 'trajectory' and 'long-term earning potential' instead of addressing immediate grievances, shifts to organizational philosophy ('we're giving you an opportunity') rather than individual concerns, uses 'right?' to turn every statement into a rhetorical question that assumes consensus

## Meeting behavior
Sits slightly forward, maintains engaged eye contact but nods at the wrong moments. Interrupts gently with 'I think' or 'let's go.' Monologues about org trajectory when someone raises a concrete issue. Never directly contradicts anyone — always reframes their point as a different perspective that aligns with company vision.
